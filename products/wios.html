Overview

Freedom of movement.
No compromise on image.

DC-Air® — the Wireless Intraoral Sensor — combines Athlos Si-CMOS direct conversion technology with Bluetooth 5.0 wireless connectivity. The result is a True Wireless® intraoral sensor that delivers the sharpness of direct conversion, the comfort of phosphor plates, and the workflow speed of a corded system — without the cord.

The DC-Air® system includes the sensor, a magnetic docking station, and the complete Zero Profile® holder set — designed by doctors together with Athlos for accurate intraoral positioning and patient comfort.

Features

Built for the clinic. Engineered for precision.

True Wireless® Workflow

No intraoral cable. Bluetooth 5.0 wireless link from sensor to docking station to PC. Sensor range up to 3 metres. Reduces patient discomfort and simplifies clinical workflow.

Direct Conversion Imaging

Si-CMOS direct conversion technology converts X-ray photons directly into signal — eliminating the scintillator step. The result: sharper detail, better contrast, and more consistent images.

Zero Profile® Holders

The purpose-designed Zero Profile® holder set includes Anterior, Posterior, Bitewing, Endodontic and Occlusal holders. Precisely aligns the sensor without relying on the cable for angulation — designed for patient comfort and repeatable positioning.

Battery & Safety

Up to 300 X-rays per charge. On-sensor safety memory stores one image. Dynamic self-calibration with every capture. Rechargeable Lithium-Ion battery. Magnetic docking for reliable recharging.

IP67 & Durability

Enhanced IP67 protection. Ultrasonic-bonded PPSU enclosure — a material with exceptional hydrolytic stability and impact resistance. Built for the demanding realities of daily clinical use.

Broad Software Compatibility

The DC-Air® TWAIN module integrates with Dentrix Ascend, Eaglesoft, Open Dental, Dexis, TigerView, Romexis, Acteon, Apteryx, Dexis, XDR Radiology, and many more TWAIN-enabled imaging platforms.

Independent Evaluation

Evaluated by Clinicians Report.

Clinicians Report is an independent dental product evaluation foundation trusted by practitioners worldwide. In 2022, DC-Air® was reviewed and highlighted across two dedicated CR evaluations — a First Look in January and a comparative assessment published in March.

In the March 2022 comparative evaluation, CR scientists and clinicians observed that DC-Air® radiographs showed improved clarity and subtle detail compared to conventional CMOS sensors — and described direct conversion technology as a significant advancement.

High resolution and excellent dynamic range highlighted in the CR First Look evaluation.

Wireless image transfer and processing observed at approximately 6–8 seconds — maintaining clinical workflow speed without a cable.

Clinicians found the sensor easy to work with and more comfortable than conventional corded sensors.

Featured in a dedicated March 2022 CR publication: “DC-Air®: Wireless Intraoral Sensor with Improved Image Quality.”

Clinicians Report is published by CR Foundation, an independent non-profit dental product testing organisation. Evaluations referenced: CR First Look, January 2022; CR Comparative Evaluation, March 2022.
