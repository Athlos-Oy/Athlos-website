The Science Behind Performance

Direct conversion.
No intermediate steps.
No compromise.

Conventional X-ray sensors rely on a scintillator to first convert X-rays into visible light, which is then detected. Each conversion step introduces noise, blurring, and energy loss that limit what the system can resolve.

Athlos sensors use CdTe-CMOS and Si-CMOS direct conversion architectures — materials that convert X-ray photons directly into charge, then into digital signal. No light conversion. No phosphor layer. No unnecessary loss.

CdTe and Si direct conversion materials
Proprietary full-custom CMOS readout
Single-photon sensitivity capability
Advanced reconstruction and filter libraries
