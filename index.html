The Science Behind Performance

Direct conversion.
No intermediate steps.
No compromise.

Conventional X-ray sensors rely on a scintillator to first convert X-rays into visible light, which then enters a photodiode with limited fill factor and converts to an electric pulse . The non direct conversion of x-rays to light, light to electric pulse, introduces significant blur, signal loss (fill factor which is much less than 100%) and undetected x-rays (due to limited DQE of the scintillators).

Athlos sensors use CdTe-CMOS and Si-CMOS direct conversion hybrids that convert X-ray photons directly into electric pulses that retain point accuracy. No scattered light means no loss of point accuracy. No light conversion. No phosphor layer. No unnecessary blurriness. No signal and contrast loss.
