Who We Are

Sensor expertise, built over three and a half decades.

Athlos is a Finnish X-ray imaging technology company focused on direct conversion sensors — the technology that converts X-ray photons directly into electrical pulses, without a scintillator intermediate step. We develop, design, and manufacture imaging sensors based on CdTe-CMOS and Si-CMOS architectures for dental, medical, and industrial applications. We also offer manufacturing services and software products.

Our team has been at the centre of direct conversion imaging since its inception. In 1993, Konstantinos Spartiotis returned from his PhD at Johns Hopkins University and his contribution at CERN, and began the work that would define the field. Athlos carries that lineage forward.

Origin

We started direct conversion. In 1993.

In 1993, K. Spartiotis founded Simage — one of the earliest companies in the world working on direct conversion X-ray imaging using CdTe. The know-how developed there was foundational.

In 2001, K. Spartiotis founded Ajat Oy — which became the global standard in direct conversion CdTe-CMOS dental panoramic and cephalometric imaging. Under his leadership, Ajat reached €12M annual revenue, deployed tens of thousands of units worldwide, and was acquired by Varex Imaging in 2019 for €85M.

Athlos was founded to continue that tradition — with the same core team, the same technical depth, and a new generation of direct conversion products: DC-Air®, Ultra-Fast Scanning Sensor, and Industrial IP67 TDI, all manufactured in-house and at the VTT Micronova facility.

Facilities

Built at VTT Micronova. Supported by deep process expertise.

Athlos has full access to the VTT Micronova cleanroom facility in Espoo — one of Europe's most advanced nanofabrication environments, located 10 minutes from Athlos headquarters.

This gives Athlos the ability to control the full manufacturing chain — from CMOS wafer bumping and flip chip bonding to detector assembly and characterization — without the capital overhead of a standalone cleanroom.
